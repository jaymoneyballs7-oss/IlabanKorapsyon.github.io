Visualizing the Economic Impact of Corruption

To show how corruption intensity affects economic growth in different countries, using the Philippines, Indonesia, and Vietnam as examples, this visualization highlights the economic consequences of corruption.

Corruption’s impact on the economy is non-linear. Small increases might not have immediate effects, but after a threshold, growth drops faster.

X-axis: Corruption intensity (0–10 scale) derived from the Corruption Perceptions Index (CPI). Inverted as: 100 – CPI

Example: Philippines CPI = 33 → Inverted = 67 → Scaled to 6.7

Y-axis: Relative economic growth (0–10 scale) Baseline growth = 10.

Scenarios per country: Baseline, Moderate corruption, and High corruption.
